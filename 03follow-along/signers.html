who signed it?

famous signers

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson was the primary author of the Declaration of Independence and the third President of the United States. He was a leading figure in America's early development and a staunch advocate for liberty.
John Adams
John Adams was a key advocate for independence and served as the second President of the United States. He played a vital role in drafting the Declaration and was a prominent voice for the colonies during the Continental Congress.
Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin was a renowned polymath, inventor, and diplomat who helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War. He contributed to the drafting process and was respected for his wisdom and diplomacy.
John Hancock
John Hancock was the President of the Continental Congress and famously signed the Declaration with a large, bold signature. He was a prominent leader in the American Revolution and a symbol of resistance against British rule.
Samuel Adams
Samuel Adams was a political philosopher and a driving force behind the American Revolution. He helped organize opposition to British policies and played a critical role in the early resistance movements.
Roger Sherman
Roger Sherman was the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States, including the Declaration of Independence. He was a key figure in drafting the Great Compromise that shaped the U.S. Constitution.
